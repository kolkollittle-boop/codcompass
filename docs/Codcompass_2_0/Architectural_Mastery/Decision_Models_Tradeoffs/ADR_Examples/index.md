# ADR Examples

## Core Theory & Mental Models

- **ADR as boundary object**: aligns engineering, product, and compliance around a durable narrative of *why*.
- **Status lifecycle**: proposed → accepted → superseded → deprecated; ambiguity destroys archival value.
- **Context over prescription**: capture forces at decision time; future readers inherit reasoning, not slogans.
- **Consequences are hypotheses**: record expected downsides and review triggers—architecture learns from reality.

## Learning Path & Maturity Stages

1. **Minimal viable ADR** — Title, status, context, decision, consequences—in one page or less.
2. **Pattern library** — Templates for data ownership, tenancy, reliability tiers, and integration styles.
3. **Review cadence** — Scheduled revisits when metrics or regulations shift assumptions.
4. **System integration** — Link ADRs to roadmaps, risk registers, and training curricula—not a wiki graveyard.
