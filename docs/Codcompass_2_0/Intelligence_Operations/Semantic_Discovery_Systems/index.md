# Semantic Discovery Systems

## Core Theory & Mental Models

- **Intent surfaces**: users express goals in natural language; systems must map intent to governed retrieval and action—not raw keyword matching.
- **Authority & freshness**: ranking without provenance creates brittle trust; discovery layers encode credibility signals.
- **Evaluation beyond clicks**: satisfaction, task completion, and downstream outcomes qualify semantic systems better than vanity metrics.
- **Composable discovery**: search, browse, recommendations, and assistants share substrate—duplicated indexes imply duplicated failure.

## Learning Path & Maturity Stages

1. **Corpus & ontology hygiene** — Canonical entities, synonym management, and deprecation policies.
2. **Hybrid retrieval maturity** — Sparse, dense, and structured filters with explicit fusion rationale.
3. **Feedback loops** — Human calibration, guardrails against popularity bias, and regression detection on query cohorts.
4. **Cross-channel coherence** — Consistent answers across surfaces without conflicting canonical narratives.
