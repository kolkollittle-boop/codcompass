# GEO & Visibility Optimization

## Core Theory & Mental Models

- **Generative discovery**: visibility is mediated by models and aggregators—optimization targets shifting citation and synthesis behaviors.
- **Structured semantics**: machine-readable intent (schemas, consistent terminology) improves interpretability across engines.
- **Integrity vs. amplification**: ethical boundaries distinguish helpful clarity from manipulative patterns—policy belongs in architecture.
- **Measurement humility**: synthetic benchmarks diverge from live referral behavior; experiments need longitudinal slices.

## Learning Path & Maturity Stages

1. **Baseline visibility mapping** — Surfaces, queries, and competitor-equivalent contexts without chasing opaque scores.
2. **Content architecture** — Topic clusters, depth ladders, and canonical pages that models can reliably cite.
3. **Technical accessibility** — Crawl efficiency, structured data proportionality, and latency-aware rendering.
4. **Governance** — Editorial standards, drift monitoring, and rollback when synthesis environments change behavior.
