# Cost & Sustainability Engineering

## Core Theory & Mental Models

- **Unit economics**: allocate spend to customer or workload outcomes—aggregates hide mispriced architectures.
- **Trade-off transparency**: cheaper now often borrows from future reliability or velocity—name the lienholder.
- **Sustainability as efficiency**: energy and carbon correlate with architectural waste; optimization and ethics align when measured honestly.
- **FinOps coupling**: finance, engineering, and product share models—not isolated spreadsheets after invoices arrive.

## Learning Path & Maturity Stages

1. **Visibility** — Tagging, allocation, and anomaly detection at meaningful granularity.
2. **Optimization doctrine** — Right-sizing, tiering, lifecycle policies, and architectural simplicity before nickel tuning.
3. **Forecasting discipline** — Scenario planning for growth, seasonality, and workload shifts.
4. **Governance cadence** — Budget guardrails with exceptions workflows that preserve innovation bandwidth.
