# One-Person Business Operating System

## Core Theory & Mental Models

- **A solo operator is still an enterprise—just with zero slack.** Systems must absorb variance because there is no bench.
- **Operating rhythm beats inspiration.** Cash, delivery cadence, support load, and content leverage share one calendar; conflicts are architectural.
- **Brand is a distribution interface.** Reputation reduces acquisition cost; clarity of positioning reduces support and refunds.

## Key Patterns & Decision Frameworks

- **Single pane of finance:** Map runway, margin, and recurring obligations to product decisions—avoid treating pricing as marketing-only.
- **Ticket-shaped workflows:** Every recurring task becomes a template with inputs, outputs, and SLA to self.
- **Automation economics:** Automate only after manual repetition proves stability; premature automation encodes the wrong process permanently.

## Learning Path & Maturity Model

1. **Foundational:** Personal CRM for commitments, basic bookkeeping discipline, and defined weekly reviews.
2. **Intermediate:** Integrated stack for billing, support, and analytics with minimal context switching.
3. **Advanced:** Delegation boundaries—contractors, agents, or micro-services for creative labor—with audit trails.
4. **Principal:** Portfolio thinking across products and audiences with explicit kill criteria and compounding content leverage.
