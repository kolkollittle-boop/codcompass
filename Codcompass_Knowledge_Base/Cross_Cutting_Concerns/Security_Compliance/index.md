# Security & Compliance

## Core Theory & Mental Models

- **Security is emergent from defaults.** Policies rarely fail loudly; permissive baselines compound until incidents become existential.
- **Compliance is evidence engineering.** Auditors and regulators buy traceability—controls without artifacts are theater.
- **Threat models scale with interfaces.** Every new integration surface shifts blast radius; inventory interfaces like inventory cash.

## Key Patterns & Decision Frameworks

- **Least privilege by lifecycle:** Temporary elevation, scoped credentials, and automated revocation beat eternal admin keys.
- **Defense in depth with clarity:** Layer controls deliberately—know which layer is authoritative when signals disagree.
- **Privacy by architecture:** Data minimization and segmentation beat downstream anonymization heroics.

## Learning Path & Maturity Model

1. **Foundational:** Secure SDLC basics—secrets management, dependency hygiene, and sensible authn/z patterns.
2. **Intermediate:** Structured threat modeling tied to roadmap decisions and measurable control testing.
3. **Advanced:** Zero-trust segmentation, continuous assurance pipelines, and vendor risk programs proportional to criticality.
4. **Principal:** Resilience under adversarial pressure—tabletops, regulatory navigation, and crisis communications fused with engineering reality.
