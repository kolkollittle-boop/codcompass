# Cost Engineering & Sustainability

## Core Theory & Mental Models

- **Unit economics are architectural.** Latency optimizations that ignore dollar burn simply relocate pain to finance—or churn.
- **Sustainability includes human throughput.** Carbon and dollars matter; so does engineer fatigue from operational debt masquerading as “cheap.”
- **Forecasting beats surprise.** Cloud and AI workloads reward distributions thinking—not monthly averages alone.

## Key Patterns & Decision Frameworks

- **Chargeback/showback discipline:** Make teams see marginal costs of features; opaque bills produce malignant architectures.
- **Tiered storage & lifecycle:** Align retention to legal and product value—default infinite retention is often laziness, not prudence.
- **Efficiency guardrails:** Autoscaling limits, budget alarms, and architectural caps that force conversations before exponential spend.

## Learning Path & Maturity Model

1. **Foundational:** Tagging, dashboards, and anomaly detection on major spend vectors.
2. **Intermediate:** Workload-specific tuning—right-sizing, caching economics, batch vs interactive inference choices.
3. **Advanced:** Portfolio optimization across reserved capacity, spot strategies, and hybrid architectures.
4. **Principal:** Executive-trusted financial models tying architecture bets to runway and strategic optionality.
