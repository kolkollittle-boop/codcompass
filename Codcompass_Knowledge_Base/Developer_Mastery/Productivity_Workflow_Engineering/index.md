# Productivity & Workflow Engineering

## Core Theory & Mental Models

- **Productivity is throughput of correct decisions, not keystrokes.** Optimize for fewer context switches and clearer completion definitions.
- **Environment is part of the product.** Editor setup, scaffolding, and snippet libraries are amortized infrastructure—neglect them and every task pays tax.
- **Friction logs reveal truth.** Recurring annoyances are signals for automation, elimination, or scope cuts—not discipline lectures.

## Key Patterns & Decision Frameworks

- **Single-tasking contracts:** Time-box deep work; batch shallow work; protect calendars explicitly instead of hoping for gaps.
- **Template ladder:** Promote repeated sequences into checklists, then scripts, then services—only when variance stabilizes.
- **Tool consolidation:** Fewer sources of truth beat “best-in-class” fragmentation unless integration maturity is high.

## Learning Path & Maturity Model

1. **Foundational:** Repeatable project skeletons, sensible shortcuts, and disciplined note capture at decision points.
2. **Intermediate:** Integrated task systems linking commits, reviews, and deployments into traceable narratives.
3. **Advanced:** Personal platforms—custom tooling that respects maintenance budgets and deprecation discipline.
4. **Principal:** Team-scale workflow design—codifying conventions that multiply others without bureaucratic drag.
