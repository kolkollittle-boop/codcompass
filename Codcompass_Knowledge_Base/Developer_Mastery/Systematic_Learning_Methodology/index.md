# Systematic Learning Methodology

## Core Theory & Mental Models

- **Expertise is layered retrieval plus falsification.** Knowing facts matters less than knowing how to invalidate stale mental models quickly.
- **Curricula follow dependency graphs.** Skipping foundations produces brittle competence—flashy at demos, fragile under change.
- **Teaching is a compression algorithm.** If you cannot reconstruct an idea from first principles after a month, you learned labels—not mechanics.

## Key Patterns & Decision Frameworks

- **Deliberate practice targeting bottlenecks:** Identify failure modes (reading code, debugging networks, profiling DBs) and drill those—not comfortable repeats.
- **Spaced reinforcement:** Revisit core abstractions on real tasks; spaced repetition decks supplement—they do not replace shipping.
- **Artifact-first learning:** Prefer exercises that produce durable artifacts (repos, diagrams, benchmarks) over passive consumption.

## Learning Path & Maturity Model

1. **Foundational:** Structured notes, explicit prerequisites for each study block, and honest self-tests.
2. **Intermediate:** Project-led learning with milestones tied to measurable outcomes (latency, correctness, coverage).
3. **Advanced:** Cross-domain synthesis—mapping concepts across stacks to spot isomorphism and anti-patterns early.
4. **Principal:** Organizational learning loops—postmortems, reading groups, and curricula that scale onboarding without hero trainers.
