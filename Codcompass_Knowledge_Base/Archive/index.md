# Archive

## Purpose

Material placed here is **retained for lineage**—historical context, superseded runbooks, prior taxonomies, or experiments that should not guide current decisions without explicit caveats.

## Core Theory & Mental Models

- **Archive is not a junk drawer.** Each entry should record *why* it left the active corpus (technology shift, policy change, incorrect premise).
- **Supersession links matter.** Point readers from archived notes to the canonical replacement to prevent zombie practices.

## Key Patterns & Decision Frameworks

- **Deprecation headers:** Date, author, successor pointer, and confidence decay notes at the top of archived pages.
- **Minimal maintenance:** Fix only broken links that mislead; resist rewriting history—annotate instead.

## Learning Path & Maturity Model

1. **Foundational:** Folder hygiene—consistent naming and an index of migrations out of Archive when ideas revive.
2. **Intermediate:** Periodic archive audits tied to major taxonomy revisions.
3. **Advanced:** Automated checks ensuring no navigation surfaces link to Archive without user-visible warnings.
