# Research Notes

## Core Theory & Mental Models

- **Research is staged betting.** Early notes explore freely; later notes must converge on reproducibility and decision hooks.
- **Noise scales with novelty.** Emerging domains reward skepticism toward vendor demos and leaderboard chasing—track incentives.
- **Knowledge decays faster at the frontier.** Timestamp claims, version models, and preserve pointers to artifacts—not vibes.

## Key Patterns & Decision Frameworks

- **Literature triage:** Tag sources by independence (vendor vs academic vs practitioner) and replication status.
- **Notebook discipline:** Separate raw captures from distilled conclusions; link both for auditability.
- **Pivot triggers:** Define signals that would obsolete current hypotheses—prevents sunk-cost storytelling.

## Learning Path & Maturity Model

1. **Foundational:** Consistent citation format, hypothesis logs, and weekly synthesis rituals.
2. **Intermediate:** Mini-experiments—scripts, probes, or prototypes—that falsify branches cheaply.
3. **Advanced:** Collaboration patterns—shared notebooks with ownership and merge etiquette akin to code.
4. **Principal:** Research operating systems—pipelines from signal intake to roadmap bets with executive-readable summaries.
