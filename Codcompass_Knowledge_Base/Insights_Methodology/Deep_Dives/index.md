# Deep Dives

## Core Theory & Mental Models

- **Depth should reduce variance.** A deep dive succeeds when future decisions are faster and less theatrical—not when it produces more buzzwords.
- **Systems thinking respects delays.** Causes propagate across quarters; narratives that ignore lag mislead roadmaps.
- **Expert synthesis names unknown unknowns.** The deliverable is often a sharper question, not a comfortable answer.

## Key Patterns & Decision Frameworks

- **Hypothesis-led research:** Start from falsifiable claims about behavior, economics, or failure rates—collect evidence accordingly.
- **Primary sources first:** Prefer artifacts (benchmarks, RFCs, postmortems) over tertiary commentary when stakes are high.
- **Decision memo outputs:** End deep dives with options, trade-offs, recommended default, and explicit revisit triggers.

## Learning Path & Maturity Model

1. **Foundational:** Single-topic dossiers with citations and limits on scope creep.
2. **Intermediate:** Cross-functional dives bridging engineering, product, and finance vocabulary.
3. **Advanced:** Scenario planning under uncertainty—multiple futures with indicator dashboards.
4. **Principal:** Portfolio of investigations feeding strategy cadence—research rhythm encoded into leadership calendars.
