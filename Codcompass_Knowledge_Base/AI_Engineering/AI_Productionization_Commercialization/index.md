# AI Productionization & Commercialization

## Core Theory & Mental Models

- **Products monetize reliability, not novelty.** Buyers pay for predictable outcomes under constraints—latency, support load, compliance—not laboratory metrics.
- **AI skews unit economics.** Margin lives in inference cost, failure-handling labor, and refund risk; model capability alone does not define viability.
- **Distribution shapes the roadmap.** Packaging (API vs embedded assistant vs workflow automation) determines observability, billing granularity, and liability posture.

## Key Patterns & Decision Frameworks

- **Value metric anchoring:** Tie pricing and SLAs to customer outcomes or measurable workload reductions.
- **Progressive disclosure:** Ship narrow, excellent surfaces before broad autonomy; expand scope only when monitoring proves containment.
- **Support architecture:** Design runbooks, escalation paths, and customer-visible incident narratives—AI outages feel personal.

## Learning Path & Maturity Model

1. **Foundational:** Instrumented MVP with explicit limits, logging, and human fallback paths.
2. **Intermediate:** Packaging, entitlement tiers, and revenue-grade telemetry that separates pilot vanity from retention signals.
3. **Advanced:** Compliance-ready posture (data flows, deletion, audit), vendor diversification, and scalable onboarding.
4. **Principal:** Portfolio strategy across models and modalities with portfolio-level risk budgets and platform leverage.
